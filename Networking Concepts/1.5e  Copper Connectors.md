This provides a clear and practical guide to the most common fiber optic connector types. It explains their physical characteristics, how they lock into place, and where you are likely to encounter them.



### Fiber Optic Connector Types Cheat Sheet

This guide covers the four main fiber optic connector types you'll encounter in networking.

---

#### Common Connector Types

| Connector | Full Name / Nickname | Description | Locking Mechanism | Common Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SC** | **Subscriber Connector** <br> "Square Connector" <br> "Standard Connector" | A square-shaped connector with a durable, push-pull latching mechanism. | Push to insert. Pull to release (prevents accidental disconnection). | Data centers, general-purpose networking. Often seen in pairs (one for transmit, one for receive). |
| **LC** | **Local Connector** <br> "Lucent Connector" <br> "Little Connector" | A smaller, more compact version of the SC connector, similar in size to an RJ45 connector. It features a clip for latching. | Push to insert (clip locks). Push down on the clip to release. | High-density environments (patch panels, switches). Also commonly used in pairs, often held together with a clip for duplex connections. |
| **ST** | **Straight Tip** | A round, barrel-shaped connector with a bayonet-style locking mechanism. | Push and twist to lock. Reverse twist to unlock and pull out. | Older networking equipment, industrial settings, multimode fiber networks. |
| **MPO**<br> (MTP) | **Multi-fiber Push On** <br> (MTP is Corning's branded version) | A high-density connector that houses **multiple fibers** (typically 12 or 24) in a single ferrule. | Push to insert. Pull on the connector body to release (similar to SC). | High-speed data centers (40G, 100G, 400G Ethernet), backbone cabling, where space and fiber counts are at a premium. |

---

#### Key Takeaways & Visual Guide

- **Duplex Communication:** For standard fiber connections (SC, LC, ST), you almost always need **two fibers**: one to **Transmit (TX)** and one to **Receive (RX)** . This is why you often see these connectors clipped together in pairs.
- **High Density:** The **MPO connector** is a game-changer for density. A single MPO connector with 12 fibers can replace six duplex LC pairs, saving significant space in crowded racks and panels.
- **Locking Mechanisms are Critical:** In a dense rack environment, a cable snag could easily disconnect a service. The different locking mechanisms (push-pull, clip, bayonet) are all designed to prevent accidental disconnection.
